Appreciation Message

I want to use this medium to express my heartfelt gratitude to two extraordinary people in my life

By Ogungbemi Jeremiah

March 7, 2024

Mr. Ogungbemi Adio – A Father Like No Other

My dearest Dad, I've got so much to say and so many praises to shower on you. As I sit here, thinking about all the amazing qualities that make you who you are, my heart is bursting with love and gratitude for the most incredible dad in the world. Words feel inadequate to capture the depth of my appreciation for you. Your presence has been a constant source of strength, guidance, and unwavering love.

From my earliest memories to the present day, you have been my guiding light, my mentor, and my friend. You are the embodiment of discipline, my siblings often call you "the disciplinarian." You had your cane and spared no one who was at fault. I bore the brunt of your strict training more than anyone else because I am your only son and had to work in your shop from primary school until I graduated from high school. It wasn't easy, and at times I feared I wouldn't cope, but I learned perseverance from you. Today, I'm grateful. That training shaped me into who I am.

You taught me the importance of chasing my goals with intentionality. Honestly, who else could stick to a routine like you? Every day, you move through life with such precision that it amazes me.

We may not have had much in terms of wealth, we struggled but you gave me everything in terms of love, support, and direction. After high school, you secured a job for me with one of the companies you sewed for. That opportunity helped me save enough to write my Senior School Certificate Examination and support my admission into college.

In college, you continued to support me, sending money when you could and calling every single day just to hear my voice and say a heartfelt prayer. Your love and dedication are the foundation of my strength. I'm eternally grateful for the lessons you've taught me—lessons about morality, integrity, perseverance, and compassion.

You are a devoted Christian and a district pastor who leads by example. I remember the early morning worship songs you used to sing to wake us for devotion. You would teach the Word of God and ask open-ended questions to deepen our understanding. Even at over 70 years of age, you still turn heads, your charisma hasn't faded. You walk with the majesty of a brave soldier.

Romans 8:28
28 And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose.

May the Lord continue to strengthen you with divine health. May you live long to eat the full fruit of your labor, in joy and peace. Amen.

Your love for Mom has been a shining example of true partnership and commitment. You've shown us the power of respect, understanding, and unwavering support. I carry your values with me every day. Thank you for your guidance, your support, and your unfailing love. You are my anchor, my inspiration, and my hero. I love you beyond words. Cheers to the most amazing dad in the universe!

Bukola – A Sister, A Second Mother

My sister, Bukola, has been my backbone. Thank you for being more than just a sister—you've been a mother, a guide, and a constant source of love and support.

You've always been the 2.0 version of our mom, a perfect blend of a best friend and a mother. Even though we've had our disagreements and fights, I've always known that you've got my back. I hope you know that I've got yours too.

You ensured my school years were stress-free by putting me on a monthly stipend and checking up on me consistently. During my 300 level, when I was gravely ill and hospitalized, you stepped in completely. You bought my medications, paid for my transport, and when things didn't improve, you took me to your house in Ibadan.

For a whole week, you ran multiple lab tests, moving me from one place to another. Despite the expenses, you never once complained. When public hospitals offered no help, you moved me to a private hospital near your shop where I was admitted for three days. You made sure I ate well, brought me clothes, and nursed me back to health with pure love.

I can never forget that. Your kindness knows no bounds. In my final year, I needed a laptop for my project. Without hesitation, you sent me one from Ibadan. That laptop became a lifeline for me and my friend Taiwo to complete our project.

You are not just my sister, you're my second mother and confidant. During my NYSC in 2024, you welcomed me and made your home feel more like mine than anywhere else. You filled my life with joy, laughter, and a love I'll cherish forever. Your sacrifices, your warmth, and your unwavering presence are forever etched in my heart. I love you more than words can ever say.
